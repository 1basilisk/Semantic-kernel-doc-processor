The Harry Potter series follows the life of a young wizard, Harry Potter. In the first book, he lives with neglectful relatives and discovers he is a wizard, attending Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Harry learns about his parents' magical heritage and his connection to the dark wizard Voldemort, who failed to kill him as a baby. Harry befriends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, and faces various threats, including Voldemort’s attempt to regain power through the Philosopher’s Stone.

In the second book, Harry's second year at Hogwarts involves mysterious attacks linked to the Chamber of Secrets. He discovers he can speak Parseltongue and uncovers that Ginny Weasley was possessed by a diary containing a young Voldemort's memory. Harry defeats a basilisk and destroys the diary, saving the school.

The third book introduces Sirius Black, an escaped convict believed to be dangerous. Harry learns Sirius is actually his godfather and was falsely accused of aiding in his parents’ murder. Harry also uncovers that Peter Pettigrew, disguised as Ron’s pet rat, betrayed his parents.
